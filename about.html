Angel Ayala recently received his Ph.D. in computer engineering at the Universidade de Pernambuco, Brasil, in 2024. His research focused on autonomous planning and perception for object-goal navigation with a quadcopter. He was awarded with the 3rd in the Master Thesis competition at the Congresso Brasileiro de Inteligência Computacional (CBIC), and a Runner-Up Best Poster PhD Student at 2024 Latin American Summer School on Robotics (LACORO). One of his most notable publications appeared in the journal Artificial Intelligence Review, entitled “UAV control in autonomous object-goal navigation: A systematic literature review.” Its current research areas are robotics, reinforcement learning, and computer vision methodologies, with a strong focus on multi-agent systems.

💻 Education

Dec 2025 Ph.D. degree in Computer Engineering with CAPES scholarship. "Decoupled sensorimotor self-predictive representation for object-goal autonomous navigation with a quadcopter." Universidade de Pernambuco, Recife, Brasil.
Mar 2021 Master's degree in Computer Engineering with CAPES scholarship. ”KutralNext: An efficient multi-label fire and smoke image recognition model.” Universidade de Pernambuco, Recife, Brasil.
Jan 2019 Bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering. "Desarrollo de algoritmos de aprendizaje por refuerzo interactivo en robots móviles." Universidad Central de Chile, Santiago, Chile.
